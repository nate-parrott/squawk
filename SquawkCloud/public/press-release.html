This is Squawk: voice messaging done better

July 10th, 2014

There’s a time for texting. There’s a time for Snapchat. There’s a time for phone calls, and hell, maybe there’s even a time for Yo. But sometimes, you don’t want typing to get in the way of what you’ve got to say—you just want to fire off a quick voice message.

But ever since voicemail, sending voice messages from a mobile phone has been a clunky experience—hidden behind anachronistic touch-tone menus or buried deep inside Whatsapp and Facebook Messenger.

So we built Squawk, an iPhone app to liberate voice messaging. Tap a contact, raise iPhone to your ear, speak your message and put the phone down to send it. It’s that simple.

Like a conversation, all your messages are ephemeral, and multiple contacts can join in.

We're officially launching Squawk today (July 10, 2014), After a brief trial period featuring a couple hundred friends and family members, our team is proud to unveil a sleek, intuitive app, drawing minimalist inspiration from apps like Clear and Facebook Paper. It’s available on the iOS App Store—free, of course—and no account is needed.

“It’s voice messaging for the 21st century,” exclaimed Nate Parrott, one of Squawk’s co-founders. “Smartphones are the pinnacle of high technology — why are we still making phone calls and voicemails like we did in 1980?” [Ed.: none of the Squawk cofounders were around in 1980.]

“It’s simple,” said Joe Engelman, another of Squawk’s co-founders. “There are group chats and intuitive gestures. There are no bloated threads to dig through, and no poorly made autocorrections.”

Learn more at squawkwith.us.

About us

We’re a shadowy consortium of undergraduates from Brown University. We think tapping letters on glass isn’t a natural way to communicate. We started working together on building a better way to communicate on smartphones in Januaray 2014. A quiet pilot program was launched in the Spring, and on July 10th, 2014, the refined Squawk app for iOS was officially launched. An Android app is expected later this summer.
